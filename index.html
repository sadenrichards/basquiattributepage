SAMO

Jean-Michel Basquiat

American Artist, American Painter

1960 Jean Michel Basquiat born in Brooklyn Hospital to his mother, Matilde and his father Gerard.
1967 By age six, Jean-Michel is fascinated by drawing, and want's to be a cartoonist. His childhood drawing is encouraged by his mother, who takes him to New York art museums.
1971 Jean-Michel starts the tag “Taki 183” becomes present everywhere throughout New York City, especially in the subway, starting the modern graffiti movement. Jean-Michel noticed these works and they influenced his later use of graffiti to gain fame.
1977 Jean-Michel first develops “SAMO” comic character with friends at City As School high school.
1978 After throwing a cream pie in his principal’s face at a high school assembly, Jean-Michel runs out of school and never graduates. In June that year he runs away from home for good and spends time homeless and staying with friends in Manhattan.
1980 In June that year, an abstract graffiti work in the alternative “Time Square Show” gives Jean-Michel his first mention in the art press.
1982 In March that year Jean-Michel's 1st US solo exhibition, at Annina Nosei Gallery, gets excellent reviews. This makes a point where he supports himself from the sale of his paintings.
1983 Jean-Michel is included in Biennial Exhibition at the Whitney Museum. In August that year, he rents a loft at Great Jones Street from Andy Warhol, where he lives and works.
1985 In February that year, Jean-Michel appears on the cover of The New York Times Magazine. The article introduces him to a new audience, and is seen as a new expression in the art world. September that year, the Warhol-Basquiat collaborative show at Tony Shafrazi gallery is dissed by critics.
1986 Jean-Michel takes trip to Ivory Coast, in Abidjan for an exhibition. After returning, he joins a drug rehab program, but leaves after few weeks.
1987 In February that year, Andy Warhol dies unexpectedly after a gallbladder operation. Jean-Michel is devastated and depressed. His drug use increases, and he spends more time alone in his studio.
1988 Jean-Michel Basquiat dies at age 27 from combined drug intoxication in his Great Jones Street loft.
